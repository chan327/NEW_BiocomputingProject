About Aspirin

Aspirin is a commonly used medication to relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and lower fever. It belongs to a class of drugs known as nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). Additionally, aspirin is often used in low doses to reduce the risk of heart attacks, strokes, and blood clot formation.

Benefits

  • Pain relief
  • Inflammation reduction
  • Fever reduction
  • Cardiovascular protection

Side Effects

While aspirin is generally safe when used as directed, it may cause side effects in some individuals, including:

  • Stomach irritation or ulcers
  • Increased bleeding tendency
  • Allergic reactions
